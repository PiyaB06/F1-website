About Williams Racing

Williams Racing is one of Formula One's most iconic and successful teams. Founded in 1977 by Sir Frank Williams and Patrick Head, the British constructor quickly established itself as a powerhouse, winning multiple Drivers' and Constructors' Championships throughout the 1980s and 1990s. Legendary drivers such as Nigel Mansell, Alain Prost, Damon Hill, Jacques Villeneuve and Ayrton Senna have all driven for Williams, while engineering innovations helped shape modern Formula One. Today, under Team Principal James Vowles, Williams is rebuilding for the future with a clear long-term vision. Combining one of the richest histories in motorsport with modern leadership, the team aims to return to the front of the Formula One grid.

Tips for new fans: The team has been struggling in recent years, especially in the hybrid era. They managed to have a pretty good season in 2025 and James Vowles had promised that their eyes were on the future and that they would be a force to be reckoned with in 2026. However, the team is still struggling to find its footing and it seems that their struggles will continue. Be warned. Being a Williams fan is not for the weak of heart. But if you are a fan of underdogs, then this is the team for you. It also helps that Alex Albon and Carlos Sainz make a fantastic duo!

The Williams FW48

Built for Formula One's revolutionary 2026 regulations, the Williams FW48 represents the team's continued journey back towards the front of the grid. Combining Mercedes power with Williams' engineering expertise, the FW48 focuses on efficiency, aerodynamic performance and mechanical precision. Every component has been developed with one goal in mind: returning one of Formula One's most historic teams to championship contention.

The Williams Legacy

Few teams have shaped Formula One like Williams Racing. From revolutionary engineering innovations to championship-winning drivers, the team has left an unforgettable mark on the sport while inspiring generations of racers and engineers alike.

🏆 Championship Dynasty

Nine Constructors' Championships place Williams among the most successful Formula One teams in history.

👑 Sir Frank Williams

Built through determination and passion, Sir Frank Williams transformed a small British team into a global motorsport icon.

⚙ Engineering Excellence

Williams has pioneered countless innovations, producing some of Formula One's most technologically advanced racing cars.

🇬🇧 British Heritage

Based in Grove, England, Williams proudly represents decades of British motorsport tradition and engineering expertise.

🚀 Building the Future

Under James Vowles, Williams has embraced a long-term vision, investing in people, infrastructure and sustainable success.

❤️ Fan Favourite

Across the Formula One paddock, Williams remains one of the sport's most respected and beloved teams, with fans eagerly awaiting its return to the front.

Williams Through The Years

1977

Frank Williams and Patrick Head founded Williams Grand Prix Engineering.

1980

Williams won its first Constructors' Championship and became a dominant force in Formula One.

1992

The legendary FW14B, featuring active suspension, became one of the greatest Formula One cars ever built.

1996–1997

Damon Hill and Jacques Villeneuve delivered back-to-back Drivers' Championships during another golden era.

2023

James Vowles became Team Principal and began an ambitious rebuild focused on long-term competitiveness.

2026

Williams entered Formula One's new technical era with the FW48, continuing its mission to return to championship-winning form.

Did You Know?

🏎️ The Legendary FW14B

The Williams FW14B, driven by Nigel Mansell in 1992, is widely regarded as one of the greatest Formula One cars ever built thanks to its revolutionary active suspension and dominant performance.

👑 Sir Frank Williams

Following a life-changing road accident in 1986, Sir Frank Williams continued leading the team from a wheelchair, becoming one of the most respected figures in motorsport history.

🌟 Home of Champions

Many Formula One legends—including Alain Prost, Ayrton Senna, Damon Hill, Jacques Villeneuve, Nico Rosberg and Carlos Sainz—have raced for Williams during their careers.
