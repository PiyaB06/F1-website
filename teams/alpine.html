About Alpine

Alpine may be one of Formula One's newest team names, but its racing heritage stretches back decades through Renault's championship-winning history. Officially rebranded as Alpine in 2021, the team combines French automotive tradition with the world-renowned Enstone factory and Renault power unit expertise. Throughout its history, the Enstone team has captured multiple World Championships and developed some of Formula One's most memorable cars, including the title-winning Renault machines driven by Fernando Alonso. Today, Alpine continues its mission to blend innovation, engineering excellence and young driver development while proudly representing French motorsport on the world stage.

Tips for New Fans Alpine is a midfield team at the best of times. At the best of times you will see them battling for the the lower positions in the top 10, and occassionally they might just surprise you with a podium finish. Alpine also has a history of treating their drivers badly(Seriously Flavio Briatore is EVIL), so don't be surprised if you see a driver leave the team after a season or two(or even mid-season).

The Alpine A526

Designed for Formula One's revolutionary 2026 regulations, the Alpine A526 represents the team's boldest step yet towards returning to the front of the grid. Built around a lighter chassis, improved aerodynamics and the latest hybrid technology, the A526 reflects Alpine's commitment to innovation while embracing Formula One's sustainable future. Combining decades of Renault engineering experience with Alpine's modern identity, the A526 is designed to deliver speed, efficiency and excitement throughout Formula One's newest era.

Alpine DNA

Alpine combines French racing heritage with decades of Formula One engineering experience. While the name is relatively new, the team's foundations stretch back through Renault's championship-winning history, creating a unique blend of tradition and innovation.

🇫🇷 French Heritage

Alpine proudly represents French motorsport and continues a tradition that began with Renault's Formula One programme.

🏭 Enstone Factory

One of Formula One's most respected facilities, Enstone has produced multiple championship-winning cars over the decades.

⚙ Renault Engineering

Renault's expertise in hybrid technology and engine development remains at the heart of Alpine's Formula One ambitions.

🌍 Driver Development

Alpine continues investing in young talent through its academy, helping prepare the next generation of Formula One drivers.

💙 Bold Identity

The striking blue and pink colours have become one of Formula One's most distinctive liveries, making Alpine instantly recognisable on track.

🚀 Future Focus

Every season represents another step toward Alpine's long-term goal of returning to race-winning and championship contention.

Alpine Through The Years

1977

Renault entered Formula One, introducing turbocharged engines to the sport.

2005

Fernando Alonso secured Renault's first Drivers' and Constructors' Championships.

2006

Renault successfully defended both World Championships, cementing its place among Formula One's elite.

2021

Renault rebranded its Formula One operation as Alpine, beginning a new chapter for the French manufacturer.

2023

Alpine celebrated multiple podium finishes while continuing to develop young drivers and strengthen its long-term project.

2026

Alpine entered Formula One's new regulation era with the A526, aiming to challenge the front-running teams once again.

Did You Know?

🏆 Renault Champions

Before becoming Alpine, the Enstone team won both the Drivers' and Constructors' Championships with Fernando Alonso in 2005 and 2006.

🩷 The Pink Livery

Alpine's iconic blue-and-pink colours come from title sponsor BWT, making it one of Formula One's most distinctive and recognizable liveries.

🇫🇷 Alpine Road Cars

Alpine isn't just a Formula One team. The company also builds lightweight French sports cars, including the famous A110.
