About Mercedes

Mercedes-AMG PETRONAS Formula One Team represents one of the most dominant eras in Formula One history. Although Mercedes first competed in the 1950s, the modern works team returned in 2010 and quickly became the benchmark for engineering, reliability and innovation. From 2014 to 2021, Mercedes secured an unprecedented run of Constructors' Championships, powered by one of the greatest hybrid engines ever built and led by one of Formula One's strongest technical organizations.

New fan note: Mercedes fans experienced nearly a decade of dominance during the turbo-hybrid era. Even when they aren't leading the championship, they're rarely far from the front thanks to exceptional engineering, strategy and driver development.

The Mercedes W17

Built for Formula One's 2026 regulations, the Mercedes W17 combines advanced aerodynamics, lightweight construction and one of the most sophisticated hybrid power units in world motorsport. Designed and developed in Brackley alongside Mercedes High Performance Powertrains at Brixworth, the W17 continues the Silver Arrows' tradition of precision engineering and relentless innovation.

Mercedes Through The Years

1954

Mercedes-Benz entered Formula One with the revolutionary W196, introducing cutting-edge engineering to Grand Prix racing.

1955

Juan Manuel Fangio secured another World Championship before Mercedes temporarily withdrew from motorsport.

2010

Mercedes returned as a full factory Formula One team after purchasing Brawn GP.

2013

Lewis Hamilton joined Mercedes, beginning one of Formula One's greatest driver-team partnerships.

2014–2021

Mercedes dominated Formula One's hybrid era, winning eight consecutive Constructors' Championships.

2025

George Russell became the team's senior driver while rookie Kimi Antonelli joined the Silver Arrows.

2026

Mercedes entered Formula One's newest regulation era with the W17, continuing its pursuit of engineering excellence.

Mercedes Innovation Lab

Mercedes has built its Formula One success through relentless innovation. Here are some of the technologies that have helped define the Silver Arrows.

⚙ Hybrid Power Unit

Mercedes created one of the most dominant hybrid engines in Formula One, delivering incredible efficiency and performance.

⚡ Energy Recovery System

The ERS captures energy from braking and exhaust gases, converting it into additional electrical power during a race.

🌪 Aerodynamics

Every wing, floor and body panel is designed to maximize downforce while minimizing drag.

🧠 Race Strategy

Hundreds of race simulations help engineers determine the fastest strategy before the lights even go out.

💻 Data & Telemetry

Thousands of sensors constantly send data back to engineers, allowing the team to monitor every aspect of the car.

🔋 Sustainable Fuels

Formula One's future focuses on sustainable fuels, and Mercedes continues to develop cleaner, more efficient powertrain technology.

Did You Know?

The Silver Arrows

Mercedes' legendary nickname originated in 1934 when mechanics removed white paint from the race car to meet weight regulations, revealing the silver aluminium body underneath.

Hybrid Era Kings

Mercedes won every Constructors' Championship from 2014 to 2021, making it the most dominant run by a constructor in Formula One history.

Technology Transfer

Many innovations developed in Formula One eventually influence Mercedes-AMG road cars, improving performance and efficiency.
