What is Formula 1?

Formula 1 (F1) is the highest class of international single-seater racing. Eleven teams compete across a global calendar, each designing highly advanced racing cars capable of speeds exceeding 350 km/h. It is governed by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA), which sets the rules and regulations for the sport. Both Teams and Drivers compete for the World Championship titles, with points awarded based on finishing positions in each race.

F1 is as much an engineering competition as a racing one. Teams build highly advanced hybrid-powered cars within strict regulations and a budget cap. It has many feeder series, including Formula 2 and Formula 3, which serve as stepping stones for drivers aspiring to reach the pinnacle of motorsport. It also has F1 Academy, a series designed to give young women the opportunity to develop their skills and eventually progress towards Formula 1.

Beginning in 2026, Formula 1 introduced major technical regulations focusing on lighter cars, increased electrical power, fully sustainable fuels, and improved racing quality.
